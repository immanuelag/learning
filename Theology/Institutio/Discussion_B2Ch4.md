These questions cut to the core of John Calvin’s "darker" chapters in Book 2. Since we are looking at this from a beginner level, let’s use the logic Calvin establishes to answer each one.

---

### 1. How does God treat people who do not acknowledge God?

Calvin makes a distinction between how God treats people **legally** and how He treats them **providentially**.

* **Legally:** Since the *sensus divinitatis* (the innate sense of God) is in everyone, those who don't acknowledge Him are "without excuse." God treats them as rebels who have suppressed the truth.
* **Providentially (Common Grace):** Even if someone denies God, He still showers them with "Common Grace." This is why an atheist can be a brilliant scientist or a kind neighbor. God "treats" them with temporal blessings—food, intelligence, and success—not because they deserve it, but to show His own goodness and to keep society functioning.
* **Judgment:** However, Calvin argues that without acknowledging God through Christ, these "common blessings" actually increase a person’s guilt because they enjoyed God’s gifts while ignoring the Giver.

### 2. What does the devil mean by serving God?

This sounds like a contradiction, but for Calvin, the devil is a **"bound" servant.**

* **The Executioner:** Calvin calls Satan God’s "executioner" or "official." Satan has no power to do anything unless God allows it.
* **Unwilling Service:** Satan doesn't *want* to serve God; he wants to destroy. But God is so sovereign that He "uses" Satan’s evil intentions to fulfill His own holy purposes. For example, God might use Satan to tempt a believer to strengthen their faith, or to "blind" a wicked person as a form of judgment.
* **The Leash:** Think of a guard dog on a leash. The dog wants to bite everyone, but the owner only lets the dog move where the owner intends. Satan "serves" God by being the tool through which God executes His secret judgments.

### 3. How to understand God hardening hearts with hearts being hard by nature?

This is a "layering" of causes. Calvin explains it like this:

* **Human Nature:** By nature, our hearts are already "stone." We are already inclined to say "No" to God.
* **God’s Action:** When the Bible says God "hardens" a heart (like Pharaoh’s), it doesn't mean God took a soft heart and turned it hard. It means:
1. God **withdraws** His Spirit (leaving the person to their own natural hardness).
2. God **delivers** them over to Satan to be further blinded.
3. God **directs** their existing hardness toward a specific event to show His glory.


* **The Analogy:** If you take a piece of wax and leave it in the cold, it stays hard. If you take a fire away from it, it gets harder. God "hardens" by withholding the "warmth" of His grace.

### 4. To what extent can we will freely?

Calvin uses a very specific definition of freedom. He distinguishes between **"Coaction"** (force) and **"Necessity."**

* **No Coaction:** You are "free" in the sense that no one is holding a gun to your head making you sin. You sin because you *want* to. It is a spontaneous choice.
* **Necessity:** You are **not** free to choose God on your own. Because your nature is "poisoned," you will *necessarily* choose what is consistent with your nature.
* **The Extent:** You have the freedom to choose between "Sin A" and "Sin B" (e.g., should I be greedy or should I be proud?), but you do not have the freedom to choose "Holy Goodness" because you have no appetite for it.

### 5. To what extent are humans under the power of the devil?

Calvin describes the unconverted person as being in the **"Kingdom of Darkness."**

* **Blinding:** Satan has the power to "blind the minds of unbelievers" so they cannot see the light of the Gospel (2 Corinthians 4:4).
* **Agitator:** Satan acts as an "inner agitator," stirring up the lusts and pride that are already in the human heart.
* **Limit:** However, Satan is not a god. He cannot read minds perfectly, and he cannot force a human to do something they don't secretly desire. He "rules" only because humans willingly follow his lead.

### 6. How to understand the sinful will of man and the power of the devil?

Calvin brings these together by saying they are in **voluntary partnership.**

* **The Rider and the Horse:** Calvin uses an old analogy (from Augustine). The human will is like a horse. If God rides the horse, He steers it toward goodness. If the Devil rides the horse, he steers it toward evil.
* **The Catch:** The "horse" (the human will) *wants* to be ridden by the Devil because its nature is currently rebellious.
* **Responsibility:** Even though the Devil is "steering," the man is still responsible. Why? Because the man *consents* to the rider. The sinful will and the power of the devil work in harmony—the devil provides the temptation, and the human heart provides the "fuel" for that temptation to burn.

---

**Summary of Book 2, Chapter 4:**
Man is a "willing slave." We aren't robots, but our "factory setting" is so broken that we use our freedom to stay in the dark. Only the "Special Grace" of God can break that partnership and give us a new "Rider."
